Windows Autopatch is great until it fails, and you are left staring at cryptic hex codes and generic event logs.

We've all been there—Googling `0x80240438` only to find forum posts from 2015 telling you to run `sfc /scannow`.

This is exactly where AI can save you hours of troubleshooting. You don't need it to fix the machine; you just need it to translate the garbage output of the Windows Update agent into an actionable root cause.

I just published a new guide on how desktop engineers can safely use AI for Windows Autopatch Root Cause Analysis (RCA).

The key takeaways:
- Never dump raw, massive logs into the prompt.
- Feed the AI the specific Intune error, a targeted snippet of `Get-WindowsUpdateLog`, and your DO registry keys.
- Ask for analysis and non-destructive validation steps, not a remediation script.

AI is an incredible log parser, but you still have to be the engineer who validates the fix.

SCCM boundary group work looks boring until it quietly wrecks content location, WAN traffic, or site assignment behavior.

That is exactly why AI is useful here.
Not because it "knows SCCM better" than an experienced engineer. It doesn’t.
It’s useful because boundary validation is repetitive, messy, and full of ugly query work that most of us do not want to write from scratch.

The catch: if you let AI guess table names, joins, or ConfigMgr logic, you get a polished lie.

I published a practical guide on using AI to build read-only SCCM boundary group validation queries safely:

- define the exact validation job before prompting
- feed AI trusted schema or a known-good query skeleton
- force it to label assumptions instead of bluffing
- keep the first pass read-only
- validate against a location you already understand before trusting the report

AI is a good drafting assistant.
It is a terrible source of authority.

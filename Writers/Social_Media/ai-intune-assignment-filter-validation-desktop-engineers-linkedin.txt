Intune assignment filters are one of those features that look harmless until they quietly target the wrong devices.

That is why I think AI is more useful as a **filter reviewer** than a filter writer.

If you hand AI your filter logic, deployment context, and a few known include/exclude devices, it can do something genuinely helpful:

- restate what the filter is actually doing in plain English
- call out fragile assumptions
- flag likely false includes and false excludes
- suggest a small validation matrix before broad rollout

What it should NOT do is bless the rollout for you.

I published a practical guide on using AI to review Intune assignment filters without treating the model like a production authority:

https://zakitpro.com/ai/ai-intune-assignment-filter-validation-desktop-engineers/

If your team uses filters for app targeting, compliance scope, or policy rollout control, this is worth tightening up.

Quiet targeting mistakes are still production mistakes.

Staring at the Windows Event Viewer for an hour is a terrible way to troubleshoot.

Most desktop engineers waste entirely too much time scrolling past meaningless DCOM warnings trying to find the one Error event that actually caused a machine to crash.

Stop doing that. Use AI to do the heavy lifting.

But do not just dump a raw .evtx file into a chatbot. That guarantees hallucinations. 

The real workflow:
1. Use PowerShell to export only the Warnings, Errors, and Criticals from the last 24 hours to a CSV.
2. Feed the CSV to the AI with a strict prompt.
3. Tell it to ignore standard Windows noise and build a timeline leading up to the exact time of the crash.
4. Let the AI give you the specific Event ID and failing module.
5. You, the engineer, verify the hypothesis before taking action.

AI finds the needle in the haystack. You decide what to do with the needle.
